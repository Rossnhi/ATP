AUTOMATED THEOREM PROVER — USER GUIDE
====================================

This prover accepts first-order logic (FOL) formulas with equality.
The input is parsed, converted to Negation Normal Form (NNF),
and Skolemized before proof search.

------------------------------------
1. LOGICAL CONNECTIVES
------------------------------------

Negation:        ¬A
Conjunction:     A ∧ B
Disjunction:     A ∨ B

Implication:     A → B      (allowed, eliminated internally)
Biconditional:  A ↔ B      (allowed, eliminated internally)

------------------------------------
2. QUANTIFIERS
------------------------------------

Universal:       ∀x A
Existential:     ∃x A

Examples:
  ∀x P(x)
  ∀x ∃y R(x, y)

------------------------------------
3. TERMS
------------------------------------

Variables:
  - Must start with a lowercase letter
  - Examples: x, y, z, x1, foo

Constants:
  - Uppercase or system-generated
  - Examples: A, E, SC0

Functions:
  - f(t1, ..., tn)
  - Examples:
      f(x)
      mul(x, y)
      inv(x)

------------------------------------
4. PREDICATES
------------------------------------

Predicates are identifiers applied to terms.

Examples:
  P(x)
  Equal(x, y)
  R(f(x), y)

------------------------------------
5. EQUALITY
------------------------------------

Equality is built-in and written as:

  t1 = t2

Examples:
  x = y
  mul(x, e) = x

------------------------------------
6. OPERATOR PRECEDENCE
------------------------------------

Highest to lowest:

  ¬
  ∀ ∃
  ∧
  ∨
  →
  ↔

Parentheses may be used freely.

------------------------------------
7. SKOLEMIZATION BEHAVIOR
------------------------------------

Existential quantifiers are eliminated automatically.

Examples:

  ∃x P(x)
    → P(SC0)

  ∀x ∃y P(x, y)
    → ∀x P(x, sf0(x))

Shadowing is respected:

  ∀x (P(x) ∨ ∃x Q(x))
    → ∀x (P(x) ∨ Q(SC1))

------------------------------------
8. CURRENT LIMITATIONS
------------------------------------

- Universal quantifiers are retained after Skolemization
- No proof search yet (tableaux coming next)
- No arithmetic or theory reasoning beyond equality

------------------------------------
9. INTENDED USE
------------------------------------

This prover is designed for:
- First-order logic
- Algebraic theories (e.g. group theory)
- Educational and experimental proof search

------------------------------------
END OF USER GUIDE
------------------------------------
